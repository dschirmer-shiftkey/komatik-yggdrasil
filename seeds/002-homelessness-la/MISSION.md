## Mission Statement

Research, model, and propose evidence-based approaches to reducing homelessness
in Los Angeles County through data analysis of root causes, intervention
effectiveness, housing policy, and service delivery systems.

## The Problem

Los Angeles County has one of the largest unsheltered homeless populations in
the United States — over 75,000 people on any given night. The crisis is driven
by a convergence of factors: extreme housing unaffordability, insufficient
mental health and substance use services, inadequate shelter capacity, slow
permanent supportive housing pipelines, and systemic gaps in discharge planning
from hospitals, jails, and foster care.

Despite billions in public spending, the population continues to grow. The
problem is not a lack of effort but a lack of coordination, data-driven
prioritization, and evidence synthesis across the dozens of agencies, nonprofits,
and government bodies involved.

## Scope

This Seed investigates:

1. **Root cause analysis** — Quantify the relative contribution of housing costs,
   mental health, substance use, economic displacement, domestic violence, and
   system discharge failures to LA's homeless population
2. **Intervention effectiveness** — Evaluate what's working: Housing First,
   rapid rehousing, permanent supportive housing, bridge housing, safe parking,
   street outreach, coordinated entry systems
3. **Housing pipeline analysis** — Model the gap between housing production
   (new units, ADUs, conversions) and the population needing placement, by
   Service Planning Area (SPA)
4. **Service delivery mapping** — Map the network of shelters, navigation
   centers, mental health providers, substance use programs, and re-entry
   services across LA County, identifying coverage gaps and bottlenecks
5. **Policy comparison** — Analyze approaches from peer cities (Houston, San
   Francisco, New York, Salt Lake City, Helsinki) for transferable lessons

## Constraints

- All analysis must use publicly available data (LAHSA, HUD, Census, LACDMH)
- Recommendations must be politically neutral — present evidence, not advocacy
- Cost estimates must include realistic implementation timelines
- Solutions must work within existing legal and regulatory frameworks
- Community impact must be considered for all proposed interventions

## Out of Scope

- Federal homelessness policy advocacy
- Criminalizing or punitive approaches to homelessness
- Solutions requiring state constitutional amendments
- Individual case management (this is systems-level analysis)
- Real estate investment or development proposals

## Success Criteria

This Seed succeeds if its outputs provide LA County policymakers,
service providers, and community organizations with:
- A clear, data-backed picture of why the crisis persists
- Ranked intervention strategies by cost-effectiveness and scalability
- Actionable housing pipeline models by SPA
- A service gap map that can inform resource allocation decisions

## Geographic Focus

**Primary**: Los Angeles County (all 8 Service Planning Areas)
**Data sources**: LAHSA Point-in-Time Count, HMIS, HUD Annual Homeless
Assessment Report, LA County DCFS, LACDMH, California Housing Partnership,
US Census American Community Survey
