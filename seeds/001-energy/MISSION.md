## Mission Statement

Research, model, and prototype approaches to solving energy poverty in
sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia using distributed renewable generation,
open hardware designs, and community-managed microgrids.

## The Problem

Over 750 million people lack access to reliable electricity. In sub-Saharan
Africa, electrification rates remain below 50% in many countries. In Southeast
Asia, remote island and mountain communities remain disconnected from national
grids. Energy poverty constrains education, healthcare, economic activity, and
quality of life.

Centralized grid extension is prohibitively expensive for dispersed rural
populations. The solution must be distributed, affordable, maintainable with
local skills, and built from components available in-country.

## Scope

This Seed investigates:

1. **Distributed renewable generation** — Solar, micro-hydro, small wind, and
   biogas systems sized for community-scale deployment (10-500 households)
2. **Open hardware designs** — Charge controllers, battery management systems,
   inverters, and distribution panels that can be manufactured or assembled
   locally using widely available components
3. **Community-managed microgrids** — Governance models, tariff structures,
   maintenance protocols, and training materials for community-owned energy
   cooperatives
4. **Energy storage** — Battery chemistries and mechanical storage appropriate
   for tropical climates with limited technical support infrastructure
5. **Demand-side optimization** — Efficient appliances, load scheduling, and
   productive use applications that maximize the value of limited generation

## Constraints

- All designs must be replicable without proprietary parts
- All designs must be operable with limited local technical infrastructure
- Research must prioritize solutions appropriate for tropical climates
- Cost targets: under $500/household for basic electrification
- Maintenance must be possible with locally available tools and skills

## Out of Scope

- Grid-scale power generation (>1 MW)
- Nuclear energy
- Fossil fuel generation
- Solutions requiring specialized manufacturing facilities
- Political advocacy or policy lobbying

## Success Criteria

This Seed succeeds if its outputs enable a community of 100+ households
in a sub-Saharan African or Southeast Asian village to achieve basic
electrification using only the published designs, open hardware specs, and
governance guides — without requiring external technical expertise beyond
initial installation training.

## Geographic Focus

**Primary**: Sub-Saharan Africa (East Africa, West Africa)
**Secondary**: Southeast Asia (Philippines, Indonesia, Myanmar)

These regions were selected based on:
- Lowest electrification rates globally
- Highest solar irradiance (favorable for PV systems)
- Existing open-source hardware communities
- Active NGO and development organization presence for validation
