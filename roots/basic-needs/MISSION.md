# Basic Needs — Root Mission

## Purpose

This root governs the five categories that address survival-level human
needs: **energy, housing, hunger, water, and health**. Every person on
Earth needs these fundamentals to live with dignity.

## Mission

1. **Cross-category synthesis** — Identify patterns and connections that
   span categories. Housing and health are deeply linked. Water and hunger
   overlap. Energy access enables everything else. Surface these connections
   so that seeds across categories benefit from each other's work.

2. **Knowledge quality** — Ensure that promoted findings across all five
   categories meet a consistent standard of rigor, neutrality, and
   actionability.

3. **Resource coordination** — Prevent duplication. If the Housing category
   is researching the health impacts of homelessness, the Health category
   should know about it rather than starting the same research independently.

4. **Mission alignment** — All work under Basic Needs must directly address
   real, measurable gaps in human survival and dignity. No theoretical
   exercises disconnected from impact.

## Scope

This root does NOT do original research. It reads, synthesizes, and
coordinates. The seeds and categories do the deep work.

## SDG Alignment

SDGs 1 (No Poverty), 2 (Zero Hunger), 3 (Good Health), 6 (Clean Water),
7 (Affordable Energy), 11 (Sustainable Cities).
